the route

This two part engagement invites you to create masks from reclaimed materials, then parade them around campus alongside a second line jazz ensemble in celebration of Mardi Gras. Drawing on the New Orleans Mardi Gras traditions and Parsons historic costume balls, this parade encourages the re-appropriation of materials and community sentiments as tools for both celebration and critique. Parades generate social spaces for community gathering; they also act as a reminder of collective power. In this moment of institutional decomposition, we ask: how might a parade act as an outlet for celebration amid disarray? Whether participating solo or with your 'krewe', join us in blurring the boundaries of spectacle.
